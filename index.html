About Me

Hello, I am Lachlan Watts-Tobin. I am a third-year EECS (Electrical Engineering & Computer Sciences) student at UC Berkeley, pursuing a minor in Mechanical Engineering, who is passionate about digital design and hardware. I am also pursuing the Sutardja Certificate in Entrepreneurship & Technology (SCET) and the Berkeley Certificate in Design Innovation (BCDI).

I am currently a Hardware Engineering Intern at Pano AI, where I have redesigned the electrical layout of edge-computer systems, developed a real-time computer-vision algorithm for lens-contamination detection, and authored the standard operating procedures and manufacturing plan to scale their production. I am also the Co-Founder and Chief Electrical Engineer of Watts & Cseh Electronics, where I design and build analog circuits for guitar effects pedals inspired by vintage hardwired models. On campus, I develop carbon-fiber manufacturing techniques as a CFRP Engineer with Bike Builders of Berkeley, and I design PCB motor controllers and write their firmware as an Electrical Engineer on the Cal Solar Vehicle team. Previously, I was a remote Hardware Engineering Intern at Vision IV in Yokohama, Japan, and I taught pre-calculus to roughly 100 incoming engineering students as a Mathematics Instructor with UC Berkeley's College of Engineering.
